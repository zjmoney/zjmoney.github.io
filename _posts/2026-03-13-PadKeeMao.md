A lighter take on drunken noodles that captures the essence of Pad Kee Mao without the heavy carbs. Rice paper stands in for traditional rice noodles and monk fruit replaces sugar, bringing this classic Thai dish down to cutting-friendly macros.

| Macros       | Timing           | Servings|
|--------------|------------------|---------|
| 515 calories | Prep: 10 minutes | Prep: 1 |
| 51g protein  | Cook: 10 minutes | Cook: 1 |


## Why it works
This recipe captures the essence of Pad Kee Mao with elements of a drunken noodle without any of the heavy carbs from traditional rice noodles. We replace the noodles with rice paper, cutting it wide to simulate the same experience. By stacking the rice paper, we also give the noodles more thickness than would normally come from pure rice paper. For the sauce, we substitute monk fruit sweetener for traditional sugar, creating a taste profile that is almost the same with 0 calories.

## Ingredients

- [ ] Chicken _(Per Serving)_
- - [ ] 180g chicken breast, thinly sliced _(215 cal, 43g protein)_
- - [ ] 8g fish sauce _(5 cal)_
- - [ ] 5g rice vinegar

- [ ] Sauce _(Per Serving)_
- - [ ] 15g oyster sauce _(8 cal)_
- - [ ] 7.5g light soy sauce _(4 cal)_
- - [ ] 7.5g dark soy sauce _(4 cal)_
- - [ ] 8g monk fruit sweetener
> The monk fruit sweetener used here is Lakanto Monk Fruit Sweetener Golden.
{: .prompt-info }
- - [ ] 2g white pepper

- [ ] Sauté _(Per Serving)_
- - [ ] 1 Thai chili, finely chopped
- - [ ] 3 Chinese broccoli _(15 cal, 1g protein)_
- - [ ] 1 tomato _(10 cal)_
- - [ ] 10g garlic _(15 cal)_
- - [ ] 1 egg _(80 cal, 7g protein)_

- [ ] Noodles _(Per Serving)_
- - [ ] 4 square rice papers _(160 cal)_

> If using round rice papers, use 5 instead of 4.
{: .prompt-info }

## Prep
#### Chicken
1. Thinly slice the chicken breast. Combine with fish sauce and rice vinegar in a bowl and set aside.

#### Sauce
1. Combine oyster sauce, light soy sauce, dark soy sauce, monk fruit sweetener, and white pepper in a bowl and set aside.

#### Vegetables
1. Finely chop the Thai chili.
2. Separate the Chinese broccoli into stems and leaves. Cut the stems into bite-sized pieces.
3. Cut the tomato into wedges.

#### Noodles
1. Wet a cutting board, then soak rice papers in sets of two, stacking them on top of each other.
2. Cut the soaked, stacked rice papers into wide strips.

> Soaking in sets of two and stacking them gives the noodles more thickness and a better texture. A wet cutting board prevents the rice paper from sticking.
{: .prompt-tip }

## Cook
1. Heat pan, then add garlic and chili.
2. Add Chinese broccoli stems and cook for 30 seconds.
3. Add chicken and cook for 2-3 minutes.
4. Add broccoli leaves, then lower heat.
5. Scramble egg in the pan.
6. Add cut tomatoes.
7. Add sauce, then add noodles.
8. Sauté noodles until everything is evenly cooked, adding water as necessary to prevent noodles from drying out.
