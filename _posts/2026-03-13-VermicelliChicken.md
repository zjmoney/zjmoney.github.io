## At a Glance
Sweet and spicy Vietnamese vermicelli bowls that come together in under 10 minutes once the prep is done. The marinade, pickling, and sauces are made in bulk, so multiple servings can be assembled quickly throughout the week.

| Macros       | Timing           | Servings|
|--------------|------------------|---------|
| 455 calories | Prep: 20 minutes | Prep: 8 |
| 50g protein  | Cook: 10 minutes | Cook: 1 |

> Prep time is per batch of 8 servings. The chicken marinade and pickled vegetables each require at least 24 hours before use.
{: .prompt-warning }


## Why it works
We replace the normal sugars in this sweet and spicy dish with a collection of zero calorie sweeteners to give the same taste at a fraction of the calories. For the marinade, we mix both allulose (which browns like real sugar but is not as sweet), and monk fruit (which tastes more like sugar) to achieve the same effect. We extend this to the nuoc cham and pickling as well, which generally use high amounts of sugar. To add an additional layer of spice and complexity, we also introduce a green cilantro sauce. While this recipe has a lot of prep, it can be put together in only 5-10 minutes afterwards, making for a quick meal at any time.

## Ingredients

- [ ] Chicken _(Per Serving)_
- - [ ] 200g chicken breast _(220 cal, 46g protein)_

- [ ] Marinade _(4 Servings)_
- - [ ] 24g allulose
- - [ ] 15g monk fruit sweetener
> The monk fruit sweetener used here is Lakanto Monk Fruit Sweetener Golden. Allulose is used alongside it because it browns properly like real sugar, while monk fruit provides the sweetness.
{: .prompt-info }
- - [ ] 15g dark soy sauce _(15 cal)_
- - [ ] 45g fish sauce _(30 cal)_
- - [ ] 15g light soy sauce _(10 cal)_
- - [ ] 10g neutral oil _(85 cal)_
- - [ ] 24g brown sugar _(91 cal)_
- - [ ] Dash of lime juice

- [ ] Cilantro Sauce _(8 Servings)_
- - [ ] 1 bunch cilantro
- - [ ] 2 jalapeños _(5 cal)_
- - [ ] 60g light mayo _(140 cal)_
- - [ ] 30g garlic _(30 cal)_
- - [ ] 15g lime juice
- - [ ] 60g 0% greek yogurt _(35 cal)_
- - [ ] Dash of salt

> You can also add Vietnamese mint to add more complexity to the sauce.
{: .prompt-tip }

- [ ] Toppings _(Per Serving)_
- - [ ] Chopped lettuce
- - [ ] Diced cucumbers _(10 cal)_
- - [ ] Pickled carrots and daikon _(10 cal)_

- [ ] Base _(Per Serving)_
- - [ ] Rice vermicelli noodles _(130 cal)_

- [ ] Pickling Liquid _(8+ Servings)_
- - [ ] 50g monk fruit sweetener
- - [ ] 200g warm water
- - [ ] 100g rice vinegar

- [ ] Nuoc Cham _(8-12 Servings)_
- - [ ] 15g monk fruit sweetener
- - [ ] 30g water
- - [ ] 15g fish sauce _(10 cal)_
- - [ ] 15g rice vinegar
- - [ ] 15g lime juice
- - [ ] Minced garlic
- - [ ] Thai red chili pepper

> Nuoc cham amount varies based on the eater's preference for spice and acidity.
{: .prompt-info }

## Prep
#### Chicken Marinade
1. Combine all marinade ingredients in a bowl and mix well.
2. Add 4 servings of chicken breast to the marinade and refrigerate for at least 24 hours.

> Additional chicken can be portioned and frozen in the marinade for later use.
{: .prompt-tip }

#### Pickled Vegetables
1. Thinly slice carrot and daikon.
2. Sit in salt for 15 minutes, then drain. Wash the salt water out and squeeze out any remaining water.
3. Dissolve monk fruit sweetener in warm water, then add rice vinegar.
4. Submerge the vegetables in the pickling liquid and refrigerate for at least 24 hours.

#### Cilantro Sauce
1. Finely chop the cilantro, jalapeños, and garlic.
2. Combine with light mayo, greek yogurt, lime juice, and salt. Mix well.

#### Nuoc Cham
1. Dissolve monk fruit sweetener in water.
2. Add fish sauce, rice vinegar, and lime juice. Mix well.
3. Add minced garlic and finely chopped Thai red chili pepper.

#### Cucumbers
1. Dice English or Japanese cucumbers into bite-sized pieces.
2. Mix with a splash of rice vinegar.

> Cucumbers can be prepped 4 servings at a time.
{: .prompt-info }

## Cook
1. Heat a pan to high heat. At the same time, fill a small pot with water and bring to a boil.
2. Pan fry the chicken until golden brown, then reduce to medium heat to finish cooking.

> Cover the pan to help keep the juices in while cooking.
{: .prompt-tip }

3. As the chicken cooks, add one serving of vermicelli noodles to the boiling water, then turn off the heat.
4. Allow the rice noodles to soak for 60-90 seconds depending on the brand, then strain and pat dry with a paper towel.

> You can shock the noodles with a douse of cold water to stop the cooking immediately.
{: .prompt-tip }

5. Place the rice noodles in a serving bowl, then add cucumber, pickled carrots and daikon, and 1/8th of the cilantro sauce.
6. Slice the chicken and place on top. Sprinkle nuoc cham over the bowl, then mix and enjoy.
